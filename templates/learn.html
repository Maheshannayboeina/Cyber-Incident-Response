Learn About Crime Reporting

Explore resources to better understand crime reporting, your rights, and how to stay safe.

Why Report Incidents?

Reporting incidents is a crucial part of making our communities safer. It helps law enforcement understand the types of crimes occurring, identify patterns, and deploy resources effectively. Your report, even if seemingly minor, can contribute to preventing future incidents.

Understanding Different Types of Crimes

Being aware of different types of crimes helps you identify and report incidents accurately:

  • Theft: The unlawful taking of someone else's property.
  • Assault: Physical harm or threat of harm to another person.
  • Fraud: Deceiving someone for financial gain.
  • Cybercrime: Crimes committed using computers or the internet.
  • Domestic Violence: Abuse within a family or household.
  • Hate Crimes: Crimes motivated by prejudice or bias.

Your Rights as a Reporter

You have the right to report an incident without fear of retaliation. Depending on the nature of the crime, you can often report anonymously. Your information is generally kept confidential to ensure your safety and the integrity of the reporting process.

How to Report an Incident

When reporting, provide as many details as possible:

  • What happened? Clearly describe the incident.
  • Where did it happen? Provide the location as accurately as possible.
  • When did it happen? Note the date and time.
  • Who was involved? Describe the individuals, if applicable.
  • Any evidence? Note if you have photos, videos, or other evidence.
